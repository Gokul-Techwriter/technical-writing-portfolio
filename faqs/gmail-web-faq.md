## 4. General FAQs

**Q1. What is Gmail (Web version)?**

**A:** Gmail (Web version) is the browser-based version of Gmail developed by Google that allows users to send, receive, and organize emails using a web browser.

**Q2. Which operating systems are supported for using Gmail (Web version)?**

**A:** Gmail (Web version) can be accessed on any operating system that supports a modern web browser, including Android, iOS, Windows, macOS, Linux, and ChromeOS.

## 5. Account & Login FAQs

**Q1. How do I log into Gmail (Web version)?**

**A:**
1. Open **Gmail (Web version)** on any browser
2. Enter your **email address** > click **Next**
3. Enter your **password** > click **Next**
4. Click **Sign in**

**Q2. Why can't I log in to my account?**

**A:** Ensure the system has a stable Internet connection and confirm the entered credentials are correct.

## 6. Email Sending & Receiving FAQs

**Q1. How do I send an email through Gmail (Web version)?**

**A:**
1. Click **Compose**.
2. Enter the recipient's email address in the **To** field.
3. Enter the subject and message.
4. Click **Send**.

**Q2. How do I view the messages I received?**

**A:** Incoming emails are displayed in the Inbox folder by default.

## 7. Features FAQs

**Q1. How do I change the maximum page size?**

**A:**
1. Click **Settings**.
2. Select **See all settings**.
3. Under the **General tab**, locate **Maximum page size**.
4. Select the preferred number of conversations per page.
5. Click **Save Changes**.

**Q2. How do I archive a mail?**

**A:**
1. Select the mail you want to archive
2. Right-click and select **Archive**

## 8. Troubleshooting FAQs

**Q1. Why does my email fail to send?**

**A:** Mail failures may occur due to an incorrect address or an unstable Internet connection. Verify the recipient's email address and check your Internet connection.

**Q2. Why can't I receive emails?**

**A:** Email receiving failure may occur due to insufficient space on the Google Account. Delete unnecessary emails and permanently remove them from the **Trash** to free up storage space.

## 9. Privacy & Security FAQs

**Q1. Is user data encrypted in Gmail (Web version)?**

**A:** Yes, in Gmail (Web version), user data is encrypted in transit using HTTPS (TLS) and encrypted at rest on Google's servers to ensure security.

**Q2. How is personal information protected in Gmail (Web version)?**

**A:** Personal information in Gmail (Web version) is protected through HTTPS (TLS) encryption, data encryption at rest, spam and phishing protection, and advanced security systems managed by Google.
