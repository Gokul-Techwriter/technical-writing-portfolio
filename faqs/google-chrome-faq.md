## 4. General FAQs

**Q1. What is Google Chrome?**

**A:** Google Chrome is a free web browser developed by Google that allows its users to access websites on the internet.

**Q2. Which operating systems are supported for using Google Chrome?**

**A:** Operating systems that support Google Chrome:

- On mobile:
  - Android (Android 8.0 or later)
  - iOS (iOS 16 or later)
- On desktop:
  - Windows (Windows 10 or later)
  - macOS (macOS 10 Big Sur or later)
  - Linux (64-bit Linux distribution with glibc 2.17 or later)

## 5. Installation FAQs

**Q1. How do I install Google Chrome on Windows?**

**A:**
1. Go to the official website of Google Chrome.
2. Click **Download Chrome** and wait for the download to complete.
3. Run the installer file to install Google Chrome on your system.

**Q2. Why does installation fail?**

**A:** Installation failure happens due to insufficient disk space, a corrupted installer file, and an outdated OS.

## 6. Account & Login FAQs

**Q1. How do I log in to Google Chrome?**

**A:**
1. Click the **Profile icon** in the top right corner
2. Click **Sign in to Chrome**
3. Enter your **email address** > click **Next**
4. Enter your **password** > click **Next**

**Q2. Why am I unable to log in?**

**A:** Ensure a stable internet connection and verify that the entered credentials for login are correct.

## 7. Feature FAQs

**Q1. How do I set Chrome as the default browser?**

**A:**
1. Open **Chrome** > **Settings** > **Default browser**
2. Click **Make Default** to set Chrome as the default browser.

**Q2. How do I clear the cache?**

**A:** For clearing cache:

- On desktop (Windows/macOS/Linux):
  1. Open **Chrome** > **Settings**
  2. Go to **Privacy and security** > **Clear browsing data**
  3. Select **Cached images and files** > Click **Clear data**

- On mobile:

  Android:
  
  1. Open **Chrome**
  2. Tap on the **three-dot menu** (**⋮**) > **Settings**
  3. Tap **Privacy and security** > **Clear browsing data**
  4. Select **Cached images and files**
  5. Tap **Clear data**

  iOS:
  
  1. Open **Chrome**
  2. Tap on the **three-dot menu** (**⋮**) > **Settings**
  3. Tap **Privacy** > **Clear Browsing Data**
  4. Select **Cached Images and Files**
  5. Tap **Clear Browsing Data** to confirm

## 8. Troubleshooting FAQs

**Q1. Chrome fails to update. Why?**

**A:** Update failure occurs due to insufficient disk space. Ensure a stable internet connection > confirm there is enough disk space for the update; if not, clear some space.

**Q2. Why is the application running slowly?**

**A:** Slow performance may occur due to too many 
open tabs, outdated Chrome version, or insufficient 
system memory. Try closing unused tabs, clearing 
cache, or updating Chrome via **Settings** > 
**Help** > **About Google Chrome**.

## 9. Security & Privacy FAQs

**Q1. Is user data encrypted?**

**A:** Google Chrome uses encryption, sandboxing, Safe Browsing, and automatic updates to protect user data.

**Q2. How is personal information protected?**

**A:** Personal information in Google Chrome is protected through encryption, sandboxing, Safe Browsing security checks, automatic updates, and secure account authentication, like two-step verification.
